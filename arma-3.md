# 🚁 Arma 3 helicopter guide for regular players

**By j [heli pilot]**

Arma 3 helicopters are not usual fps helicopters.

They punish overcorrection, bad landing zones, and impatient flying much harder.  
The good news is that Arma 3 gives you a very clean way to learn them step by step: the **Virtual Reality terrain / VR training content**, 
the **official helicopter VR training missions**, and then free practice in the editor before you ever try public multiplayer.

## Best path to learn helicopters in Arma 3

### 1. Start in VR, not in multiplayer
Open the **Virtual Reality** terrain and use it like a garage or test field.

It is flat, clean, distraction-free, and perfect for learning takeoff, hover, yaw correction, and landing without terrain clutter. 
Arma 3’s VR terrain is essentially a blank training space, and the official helicopter update also added **VR helicopter training** specifically for this purpose.  
Source: https://armedassault.fandom.com/wiki/Virtual_Reality_(terrain)  

### 2. Learn one helicopter first
Do not jump between five aircraft on day one.

Pick one helicopter, learn:
- takeoff
- hover
- controlled turns
- shallow approaches
- safe landings
- emergency go-arounds

In Arma 3, consistency matters more than style.

### 3. Then move to heavy lift
After basic transport flying, move to the **CH-67 Huron** or **Mi-290 Taru**.

Those two are central Arma 3 helicopter platforms, especially if you want to learn proper transport habits and **sling loading**. The Helicopters DLC specifically highlights both aircraft and sling loading as core content.  
Source: https://www.bohemia.net/games/arma3-helicopters

### 4. Only then touch advanced flight model
If you want the harder version, switch to the **Advanced Flight Model** later.

Arma 3’s advanced helicopter model is powered by **RotorLib**, and it is much better once you already understand normal hover, landing, and recovery.  
Source: https://www.bohemia.net/games/arma3-helicopters

### 5. Leave VTOLs for later
Do not begin with tilt-wing / VTOL aircraft.

The **V-44 X Blackfish** and **Y-32 Xi’an** are cool, but they are a different learning problem. Treat them as a later step after you already feel comfortable with normal helicopters.

The Y-32 Xi’an is a VTOL aircraft from Arma 3 Apex, and there are dedicated tutorials for VTOL/Blackfish flying because the control style is different enough to deserve separate learning.  
Source: https://armedassault.fandom.com/wiki/Y-32_Xi%27an

## Good helicopters to practice with

- **MH-9 Hummingbird** — light, simple, good for basic control
- **UH-80 Ghost Hawk** — safer all-round transport practice
- **CH-67 Huron** — heavy lift, transport, good habits
- **Mi-290 Taru** — heavy utility helicopter, great for sling-load mindset
- **V-44 X Blackfish** — NATO VTOL / tilt-wing style aircraft
- **Y-32 Xi’an** — CSAT VTOL / tilt-wing style aircraft

Source: https://www.bohemia.net/games/arma3-helicopters

## The real Arma 3 rule

Your first goal is **not** to become aggressive.

Your first goal is:
- take off cleanly,
- hover without panic,
- land without drifting everywhere,
- and leave the LZ alive.

That is already real progress in Arma 3.

## tl;dr;

Best learning route:

- **VR terrain first**
- **official VR helicopter training**
- **one light helicopter**
- **then Huron / Taru**
- **then sling loading**
- **then advanced flight model**
- **VTOLs last**
